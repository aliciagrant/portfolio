dance marathon

problem

Dance Marathon at the University of Florida participants needed a way to consolidate managing their fundraising efforts, tracking goals, earning spirit points, and staying updated on events and meetings. The DM at UF app aims to streamline these processes, fostering a more efficient and collaborative experience for its members.

details

  • tools: Figma, React Native
  • duration: 10 weeks
  • design process: 6 weeks
  • role: UI Designer
  • skills: Prototyping, Figma

context

The DM at UF app was expected to be designed, developed, and fully functional within just three months to support over 1,500 members, making the project timeline and scalability critical factors. We began by looking at the types of users that would be using the application.

emerging designs

With tangible users and their concerns in mind, a base for the application was made. As a captain on the digital marketing team, I presented the rough sketches to my overall director and began designing with intent of development.

iterative design

Due to the time crunch and the high expectations, I opted out of formal testing and utilized iterative design. Each captain meeting I attended, I had my team go through the prototype and give feedback. This cycle continued until refinements were launched.

reflection

The app was soft-launched on the day of the main event—April 13, 2024—with over 400 users. It performed effectively and the designs were well received by miracle makers, captains, and directors. As a designer, I gained real-world experience in delivering under a tight deadline.
